Stories of Women in the Music Industry Facing Discrimination on All Levels


Taylor Swift

Fun Facts about the Artist

  • Ranked at Number 2 in Spotify
  • Has broken 118 Guinness World Records!
  • Has 60 million subscribers in Youtube

Taylor Swift, in an interview for Vogue's September issue, discussed her experiences with sexism in the music industry. Initially, as a teenager, Swift didn't notice the unfair treatment, attributing this to being perceived as a child by industry men. However, as she matured and her success became more formidable, attitudes shifted. Swift highlighted the infantilization of her early success and the changing perceptions when she started playing stadiums and was seen as a woman. Supporting research indicates that women in the workplace face similar challenges, with men feeling threatened by female managers and women needing to prove their competence more. Swift also criticized the double standards in songwriting, where men aren't judged for using personal relationships as inspiration. Her upcoming album, "Lover," includes a song titled "The Man," which addresses these feminist themes, imagining how she would be perceived if she were a man.

Nicki Minaj

Fun Facts about the Artist

  • Ranked at Number 57 in Spotify
  • Has a tattoo in Chinese on her left arm meaning "God is with me always"
  • More of a Dog Lover than a Cat Lover

Nicki Minaj recently addressed sexism in the music industry on Twitter, prompted by rapper Russ's call for more respect for her. Retweeting Russ's video, Nicki highlighted the double standards women face, asserting that they must work twice as hard for half the recognition men receive. She questioned why male artists frequently collaborate with each other but often only work with women under pressure, referencing her own collaborations. Nicki criticized the industry for diminishing her decade of success and pointed to a 2013 tweet by Kendrick Lamar, who noted the disproportionate animosity towards her from men, to underscore her point. Nicki's candid reflections emphasize the need for the music industry to support and respect female artists.

Katy Perry

Fun Facts about the Artist

  • Ranked at Number 55 in Spotify
  • Has played roles in the acting industry
  • Has frequently practiced transcendental meditation

Katy Perry has actively spoken out against sexism, particularly within the music industry, highlighting the double standards and challenges women face compared to their male counterparts. Through her empowering music and public statements, she advocates for equal treatment and gender equality. Perry acknowledges the harder work required for female artists to prove themselves and supports initiatives aimed at dismantling sexist attitudes. Her advocacy emphasizes the importance of systemic change and the empowerment of women, reflecting her commitment to addressing and combating sexism in the industry.

Bjork

Fun Facts about the Artist

  • A Icelandic citizen
  • Her first album, "Debut", was rated platinum in the US
  • The name "Bjork" means birch, the tree

In an open letter to the media, Björk addresses the sexism female artists face regarding how their work is received. Following critiques of her DJ sets at Houston's Day For Night Festival for "hiding" behind desks, she highlights the double standards that don't apply to male DJs. Björk argues that women in music are often confined to singing about relationships, while men have the freedom to explore diverse topics without criticism. She recounts how her albums Volta and Biophilia, which tackled unconventional subjects, faced media resistance until Vulnicura, where she sang about heartbreak. Björk calls for 2017 to be a year of transformation, advocating for the right to artistic variety for women.

Rina Sayawama

Fun Facts about the Artist

  • Her music is mainly influenced by LGBTQ+ music
  • Her debut album, Sawayama, received a score of 83 by users in AOTY
  • Was included in the Vogue Japan's "Women of the Year 2019"

Rina Sawayama has faced severe racist and sexist abuse since mid-2023, describing experiences of being gaslit, disrespected, ignored, and cyber-bullied for calling out discrimination. She observes how men in the industry often discredit her and others, despite progress in attitudes towards artists' mental health. Sawayama highlights the misuse of mental health rhetoric to disguise misogyny and calls for systemic change. She urges more voices to speak against the persistent racism and sexism, advocating for a shift from competition to community among female artists to create a safer, fairer industry for future generations.

Lily Fontaine

Fun Facts about the Artist

  • Lily Fontaine's gig with the band English Teacher was next door to a female wrestling tournament
  • Is the lead singer of English Teacher
  • Their first song in English Teacher is "The World's Biggest Paving Slab" Weird!

Lily Fontaine recounts her harrowing experience working a freelance shift at Leeds First Direct Arena, where she faced immediate and relentless sexual harassment from a senior male crew member. Despite her excitement about exploring the music industry, this experience deterred her from returning, as she was advised to stay silent about the abuse, a decision she now regrets. As a Black female musician, Fontaine has faced persistent patronization, dismissal of her ideas, and gaslighting of her concerns, often in public without any support. She highlights the systemic issues in the industry, emphasizing how it devalues its hardest working members, particularly women, working-class women, and women of color, both financially and emotionally.
