Stories of Women Facing Discrimination on All Levels

Liam James Payne, an English singer, born on August 29, 1993, rose to fame and success as a member of the popular band 'One Direction'. After the fateful goodbye of the band, Payne signed a solo contract with Republic Records in October 2016, following in the footsteps of his fellow member, Harry Styles. His many songs, including his debut single "Strip That Down" became hits worldwide, and streamed by many.

Jessie Ware, an English singer and songwriter, born on October 15, 1984, gained recognition with her debut album "Devotion" in 2012, which received critical acclaim and was nominated for the Mercury Prize. She initially worked as a backing vocalist for Jack Peñate and collaborated with SBTRKT before launching her solo career. Some of her most notable hits include "Wildest Moments" and "Say You Love Me."

Kero Kero Bonito, a British indie pop band formed in 2013, consists of vocalist Sarah Midori Perry and producers Gus Lobban and Jamie Bulled. They gained a cult following through their unique blend of J-pop, dancehall, and electropop, particularly after their 2016 album "Bonito Generation." Their hit singles "Flamingo" and "Trampoline" helped solidify their position in the indie music scene.

SPELLLING, the stage name of Chrystia Cabral, is an American experimental pop musician who began her career with the release of her debut album "Pantheon of Me" in 2017. She gained attention for her ethereal soundscapes and unique approach to pop music, which was further showcased in her critically acclaimed 2019 album "Mazy Fly." Her standout tracks include "Under the Sun" and "Haunted Water."

Japanese Breakfast is the solo project of Korean-American musician Michelle Zauner, who gained prominence with her debut album "Psychopomp" in 2016, written after the death of her mother. Her rise continued with the release of "Soft Sounds from Another Planet" in 2017 and "Jubilee" in 2021, both of which received widespread critical acclaim. Key tracks like "Be Sweet" and "Road Head" have contributed to her growing fanbase.

Caroline Polachek, an American singer and songwriter born on June 20, 1985, initially gained fame as the lead vocalist of the indie pop band Chairlift. She embarked on a solo career in 2019 with the release of her album "Pang," which was praised for its innovative production and her distinctive voice. Her singles "So Hot You're Hurting My Feelings" and "Door" are among her most popular tracks.

Björk, an Icelandic singer, songwriter, and actress born on November 21, 1965, achieved international fame with her solo debut album "Debut" in 1993. Known for her eclectic musical style and innovative videos, she has maintained a successful career with hits like "Human Behaviour," "Bachelorette," and "All Is Full of Love." Her work has earned her numerous awards and critical acclaim, cementing her status as an avant-garde icon.

Rina Sawayama, a Japanese-British singer and model born on August 16, 1990, rose to prominence with her 2020 debut album "SAWAYAMA," which blends pop, R&B, and nu-metal influences. Before her breakthrough, she released the mini-album "RINA" in 2017, gaining attention for her unique style and powerful vocals. Notable tracks like "XS" and "STFU!" have helped her garner a devoted fanbase and critical acclaim.
